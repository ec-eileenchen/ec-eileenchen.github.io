From Data to Insights

I work with the massive Vesta partnered IEEE-CIS dataset consists 435 features from merged transaction and identity information tables. A major data quality issue was the presence of missing values and features on vastly different scales. My data analytics minor provided the framework for a proper preprocessing stage, which imvolved imputation for missing values, one-hot encoding for categorical veriables, and robust standardization to ensure no feature is set up correctly for the models.


The model selection process was a lesson in empirical testing and theoretical understanding. I experimented with XGBoost, LightGBM, and the LSTM network. The LSTM's underperformance was a key learning moment. My hypothesis, due to the characteristic of the dataset, was that the sequential nature of LSTMs required longer, consistent frudulent patterns to learn from. Given the extreme small amout of fraud cases in the dataset, the LSTM struggled to find meaningful temporal dependencies, where tree-based models like LightGBM outperform at evaluating the complex relationships between hundred of features at a single point in time. Light GBM was ultimately selected nor only for its performance across key metrics but also for its computational efficiency, which was critical given the dataset's size and my hardware constraints. Learning to implement and tune these models, especially the entirely new concept of LSTMs, was a steep curve. I relied heavily on official documentation and other related blog posts, where the key insight was understandinghow to structure time-series data for LSTM input and the importance of its hyperparameters.
